Margarete earned her PhD in the APE lab and the EECB program in Spring 2022. Her research interests include population ecology, herpetology, conservation biology, terrestrial vertebrate physiology, and species' responses to climate change. She completed her M.S. with [Dr. Nancy Karraker](http://karraker.weebly.com/) on the timing of hibernation in Eastern Box Turtles. Margarete's research in the A.P.E. lab centered around understanding the environmental and climatic niche breadth of the Mojave Desert Tortoise (*Gopherus agassizii*). Margarete worked closely with the Hunter lab at Georgia Southern University, which is performing similar research on the Gopher Tortoise (*Gopherus polyphemus*). Margarete is currently a postdoctoral research associate at Cal Poly Humboldt, where she is conducting research on salmon conservation.
